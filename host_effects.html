Life history evolution, species differences, and phenotypic plasticity in hemiparasitic eyebrights (Euphrasia)

To celebrate and make accessible some of the data from the paper that has just come out in the American Journal of Botany, I thought that I would talk about a few things here. Our paper investigates different traits of the hemiparasitic Euphrasia when they parasitise different host plants. This was possible by growing many Euphrasia plants on different hosts in a common garden experiment.

We show that Euphrasia show variation in life history strategies. Some will rapidly transition to flower, while others delay flowering and invest in vegetative growth. We hope to understand some of the plasticity in Euphrasia response to different host plant species.

Four of the traits we measured on individual Euphrasia plants were (a) height, (b) number of nodes to flower (an important taxonomic trait), (c) corolla length (important in determining whether a individual will outcross or not) and (d) how long it took to flower.
